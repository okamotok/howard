Classrooms

AV System Controls

The AV System is controlled by a wall mounted touchscreen. In rooms 527 and 529 the screen is located next to the door. In rooms 451, 452 and 453 it is located on the wall next to the whiteboard.

  • The 4 icons on the top row select which source the projector will show
    • Laptop VGA - for for displaying a laptop connected with the VGA cable
    • Laptop HDMI - for displaying a laptop connected with the HDMI cable
    • Mac Mini - for displaying the lectern's built in computer
    • DVD - for using the DVD player located in the AV rack
  • The Off and On buttons in the left column turn the projector on and off. Note that after turning the projector off it can take up to 5 minutes to turn it back on
  • Below the Projector Off/On buttons is a Mute button which toggles the projector video mute. Video mute is similar to turning the projector off but you can turn it back on instantly
  • The Screen Control button will show a menu to raise or lower the projector screens
  • Controls for sound volume are on the right side column of the panel.
  • When the DVD input is selected the DVD player control buttons are shown in the middle of the panel
  • The screen button will bring up buttons to raise or lower the screens
  • The Red Off button at the bottom of the screen will turn the projectors off and raise the screens

Using a laptop with the classroom projector

The classroom lecterns have cables to connect a laptop using either VGA or HDMI ports to the projector. Note that mac laptops do not have VGA or HDMI ports and will need an adapter to connect to the projector.

  • Turn the AV system on and press the icon on the touchscreen for Laptop VGA or Laptop HDMI for the connection you will be using.
  • Connect either the VGA or HDMI cable from the lectern to the laptop. If you are using an adapter connect the cable to the adapter first and then connect it to the laptop
  • If you are using the VGA connection and will need audio connect the audio plug from the lectern cable into the headphone jack on the laptop.
  • If the laptop does not automatically display through the projector you will need to configure it to use an external display
  • Internt Access - You can either use USFWireless or there is a network cable in the lectern that can connect to the laptop.

Classroom built in computer

The lectern in each classroom has a built in monitor and keyboard that connect to a mac mini computer that has both Mac and Windows 7 operating systems on it. To user the computer turn the AV system touchpanel on and press the icon labeled Mac Mini.

  • To access the monitor and keyboard lift up the lectern cover and fold it back. Pull the top of the monitor screen up to tilt it so you can see it. The keyboard and trackpad are located in a drawer on the bottom of the lectern.
  • The monitor has a green light on the bottom edge of it when it is turned on. If the light is not on press the power button located on the bottom edge of the monitor.
  • If the screen is blank or has a 'no signal' message you will need to press the mac mini power button which is located in the AV rack. For fourth floor rooms the AV rack is located in a cabinet in the back of the room. For 5th floor classrooms it is located in the cabinet underneath the chalkboard closest to the door The power button has a yellow label next to it
  • Choosing mac or windows - When the computer starts up it displays a menu where you can choose to use either Mac or Windows. If at anytime you need to switch between Mac or Windows restart the computer to bring up this menu.
  • Connecting a USB device There is a USB connection to the built in computer on the lectern to the right of the monitor. If you need to connect more than one device there are two more USB connections in the AV rack

Classroom DVD Player

Each classroom had a DVD player in the AV rack. For fourth floor rooms the AV rack is located in a cabinet in the back of the room. For 5th floor classrooms it is located in the cabinet underneath the chalkboard closest to the door.

  • Turn on the AV system and press the icon for DVD Player
  • The DVD player is located in the top row of the AV rack
  • If the power indicator light is orange press the Standby/On button
  • The tray that holds the disc has a DVD video logo on it and opens by pressing the square button to the right of it.
  • The controls for the DVD player are located on the wall mounted touchscreen control panel.

Group Study Rooms

Bloomberg Computers

Each group study room has a computer with Bloomberg software located in the cabinet below the display. To use the computer:

  • Turn the computer on if it is not already on
  • Check the power status light on the lower right corner of the display. If it is orange press the power button on the bottom of the display.
  • If the computer is not displaying on the display press the button on the source button on the bottom of the display repeatedly until it switches to BNC

Connecting a laptop to the display

Laptops can be connected to the groups study rooms displays using a VGA connection. Mac laptops will need a VGA adapter.

  • Check the power status light on the lower right corner of the display. If it is orange press the power button on the bottom of the display.
  • Press the source button on the bottom of the display repeatedly until it switches to PC
  • Connect the VGA connection cable in the cetner table console to the laptop
  • If the laptop does not automatically display through the projector you will need to configure it to use an external display

Lecture Capture

A lecture capture system called Echo360 is installed in classrooms 451, 452, 453, 527 & 529. The system is fully automated and records a video of the area around the lectern and a direct feed from the projector. Recordings must be requested in advance through ITS at http://itsreservations.usfca.edu. Once you have submitted a request and received confirmation there is nothing further you need to do. The system will automatically turn on and off at your requested time. After the recording completes it takes up to 2 days to process and then a link to view it will be emailed to the requestor. A valid USFConnect login is required to view the videos.

Video Camera

A digital video camera and tripod are available for recording videos. Please request a reservation for it with the reception staff or office manager in advance. After making a recording you can copy the videos from the camera to your computer and/or make a request with Kenji in ITS at x2559 to post them to a website

    To operate the camera:
  • Turn the camera by opening the viewfinder screen and pressing the power button on the side of the camera.
  • Recording - Make sure lens cover is open. If it is closed the open switch is on the side of the camera behind the lens.
  • A switch on the back of the camera changes between Record and Playback mode. Set it to the record position.
  • Battery life is indicated by an icon on the viewfinder. Use AC the adapter if the battery life is not adequate.
  • Check the record time (R) indicator on the screen. If there is not enough time for your recording delete some files from the camera.
  • To start recording press the red button on the back of the camera.
  • You can confirm recording is in progress by checking the screen for the presence of a Red circle with a running timer next to it.
  • To stop recording press the record button again.
  • Playback - Set the Record/Playback switch on the back of the camera to the Play position. Use the buttons to the left of the display screen to select and play a video
    Copying videos to a computer
  • Use USB cable to connect camera to the computer and turn the camera on.
  • On the camera display press the enter button to select PC
  • The camera will show up as a USB drive on the computer
  • Video files will be in the PRIVATE → AVCHD → BDMV → STREAM folder.
    Deleting files from the camera
  • Set the Record/Playback switch on the back of the camera to the Play position.
  • Press the trash can button on the side of the camera.
  • You can delete all videos or use Select to select which videos to delete.
  • To select videos use the arrow and Enter buttons on the side of the display.
  • When the scenes to be deleted are selected press the trash can button.

Conference Phone

A conference phone is kept at the main desk and can be setup in any of the rooms or offices at the downtown campus.

  • The phone number for it is 422-4774
  • To reserve the phone please make a request with reception or the office manager
  • For help setting up the phone and using it please check with ITS (Kenji)
  • If you need to have multiple locations call in to the conference phone follow these steps.
    1. Press the button under the box on the screen that says more
    2. Press the button under the box on the screen that says MeetMe
    3. Type in 8500
    4. The phone display should indicate To Conference (8500)
    5. Have the remote parties call 422-8500 to be added to the conference
    6. The phone will beep once when a remote party is connected and twice when they disconnect.

Telepresence / Video Conferencing

A Telepresence system is installed in room 504 that can connect to the room in Malloy Hall 405. To make a call:

  • Press the touchpanel on the table to turn on the system
  • Press the Contacts button on the bottom row of the panel
  • Press Favorites, then Malloy 405 then Call
